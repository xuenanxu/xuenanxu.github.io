Background

With 101 million enrollments in 2018, massive open online courses (MOOCs) have drastically increased access to online learning resources. However, many challenges still exist in the MOOC space, such as high attrition rates and a sense of electronic isolation.To address these problems, we designed and developed a goal-setting web application with the following objectives:

To increase learners' awareness of their desired outcomes and motivation to engage

To provide a personalized, scaffolded goal-setting experience

To afford new ways of connecting learners with each other

Overview

Goal Setter Tool is a plug-in tool for Coursera, edX and FutureLearn. It gives users the options of creating a private goal, creating a group goal or joining an existing group goal by going through a light-weight goal-setting process before taking an online course. Tailored messages are also sent via emails to users at certain launch points during the course for learners to view their recent performance, editing their goals or getting engaged in group discussions.

01. Create or Join a Goal

After confirming the email address and getting started from the landing page, users get to the Create or Join a Goal page with choices of creating a goal or joining a group goal that is already created by other users.

02. Set up a Goal

Then users walk through a light goal-setting process with 5 steps. For each of the step, we provide pre-populated content for users to choose, and references for users to get inspirations from.

03. Review Your Goal

After setting up the goal, users get to the "My Goal" page, on which the three cards reflect the users' previous input. For users who join or create a group goal, they will also be able to have discussions with other members of the same goal and check their profile. The goal can also be exported as a photo to share on social media.

Preliminary Research

Understand Online learning and goal-setting

In the early stage of preliminary research, I gathered insights from colleagues in various areas, academic literature and blog posts on goal-setting and MOOC interventions. Key findings included:

1. Goal Setting plays a significant role in MOOC learners' success;

2. E-solation (electronical-isolation) is a problem for online education;

3. Goals can be converted into social objects for increasing motivation and accountability.

Competitive Analysis

Understand the market and the competitors

To identify possible features and ways of differentiating our application, I conducted a competitive analysis of applications that focused on or incorporated goal-setting in industry, as well as online learning. The outcome of this step is a table to compare these tools. I also found that their common strenghts included using progress charts and email reminders to help users keeping track of their goals, while their shared weaknesses included the reliance on daily user input and incompatibility with the learning tools interoperability (LTI) standard, mearning they would not seamlessly integrate with learning management systems that are used to host the MOOCs we intended to impact.

Lightning Design Jam

What do we care the most

After generating personas and brainstorming, I found that we came to the situation of having too many ideas and found it hard to narrow down the scope. Thus, I run another design workshop, following the Lightning Design Jam process that was brought up by designers from AJ & Smart that helped me found the key pain points I should focus on.

The problem we prioritized in this workshop was the potential heavy goal-setting process for learners at the beginning of the course, which was rephrased into a HMW question: How might we create a lighter goal-setting process?

We came up with several actionalbe solutions :

1. Design the UI to be more conversational, gameful and playful;

2. Make sure the tool is as context-aware as possible;

3. Provide pre-populated options.

üí° What are my design decisions

Key Feature 1 | Create a private goal

For learners who are more self-motivated, the tool allows them to set up a private goal by following a scaffolded experience. We would also send email reminders at each launch point to remind learners of reflecting on their own performance.

Key Feature 2 | Create or join a grivate goal

For learners who are more socially intervened, the tool allows them to create or join a group goal to achieve the goal with other learners within the course. In the group goal, learners could share their thoughts through a discussion forum and check the performance of other learners. We would send out email reminders at each launch point to relect on their performance and check the dicussion forum if they want to.
